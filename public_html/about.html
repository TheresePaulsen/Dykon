About GBV in SA

GBV in SA is a non-profit organization based in South Africa. Its main aim is to spread awareness of the current crisis of gender-based violence (GBV) in South Africa.

Gender-based violence is a broad term, covering all forms of violence against women, children and gender-non-conforming individuals.

11 Women Murdered Every Day in South Africa

South Africa faces unprecedented high levels of violence:

  • Nearly half (48%) of citizens say GBV is a common occurrence in their community (Afrobarometer, 2023).
  • On average, 114 rape cases are reported every day, whilst many cases go unreported (defenceWeb, 2023).
  • Every day, an average of 11 women are murdered (SAPS, 2023).

Our Mission

GBV in SA aims to increase awareness of the current crisis through education. We provide educational resources to the public, as it is our vision that education leads to empowerment. Our resources range from data and statistics to prevention strategies, mitigation tactics, and awareness campaigns.

To read more about what we do as an organization, visit Our Work or explore our catalogue of Resources.
